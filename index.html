AI Effort Estimation Engine
Fiserv — production ML system
Mar 2026 – Jun 2026
Problem

Fiserv's project teams sized sprint work by gut feel. The historical record showed exactly what that cost: 57% of user stories ran over their original hour estimate, with defects overrunning by ~97% on average — and no feedback loop to learn from it.

Impact

A production-style estimation engine that gives PMs a defensible, explainable hour estimate instead of a guess, plus a real-time "Spillover Risk" check for whether a team can absorb scope changes mid-sprint — turning a recurring planning failure into an auditable process.

Email triage is slow and subjective — the same inbox produces a different "right" reply depending on who's reading it and what mood they're in. There's no audit trail for how a reply was actually decided on.

Impact

A working prototype showing that a structured debate between AI personas can produce a more defensible reply — with a clear trail of how it got there — than a single model's first draft. Directly relevant to any team that has to stand behind customer-facing communication.

LogiGAN — Logical Reasoning via Adversarial Pre-training
Applied NLP research
Jul 2025 – Oct 2025
Problem

NLP models routinely "solve" logical reasoning tasks by matching surface keywords rather than actually reasoning — they break the moment causality is flipped or a negation is inserted.

Impact

A reproducible method for stress-testing and hardening an NLP model against exactly the failure modes that make deployed language models unreliable on tasks that need real logical inference — the robustness gap that matters before any of these models get near production.

What I'd flag

Analyzing failure cases — negation, flipped causality, adversarial inputs — instead of trusting an aggregate accuracy score taught me that a model can post a good headline number and still fail predictably on one entire class of input. That's the failure mode that actually hurts in production.
